Recommendations worth packing your bags for.

Wander Log is a small, opinionated travel guide. We test the beaches, climb the temple steps, and wander the back streets so we can hand you three kinds of trips we'd take again ourselves: a beach to slow down on, a temple to stand quietly inside, and a country worth giving a full two weeks to.

01 — Beach recommendation

Railay Beach, Thailand

Reachable only by longtail boat, Railay trades beach-front traffic for limestone cliffs that rise straight out of the Andaman Sea.

Est. Beach Escape
Krabi Province · Thailand

Railay Beach

Come for the powder-soft sand, stay for the karst cliffs that turn the whole bay into a natural climbing gym. Mornings are for kayaking to the hidden lagoon at low tide; evenings are for watching longtail boats silhouette against the sunset from a beach bar built on the sand.

Est. Beach Escape
Quintana Roo · Mexico

Tulum Beach

White sand backed by Mayan ruins on one side and a strip of palm-shaded beach clubs on the other. Rent a bike to reach the quieter stretches south of town, and save an afternoon for the cenotes just inland for a freshwater break from the sun.

02 — Temple recommendation

Angkor Wat, Cambodia

The largest religious monument on earth, and still the quietest hour you can spend anywhere in Southeast Asia if you arrive before the gates open.

Sacred Temple Ground
Siem Reap · Cambodia

Angkor Wat

Arrive for sunrise and let the five towers silhouette against the sky before the crowds arrive. Spend the rest of the morning tracing the bas-relief galleries that wrap the outer walls, then cross the causeway to nearby Bayon for its hundred carved stone faces.

03 — Country recommendation

Japan

Few countries pack alpine peaks, ancient shrines, and neon-lit cities into a rail network this fast and this easy to navigate on a single trip.

Full Country Pick
East Asia

Japan

Two weeks is enough to ride the bullet train from Tokyo's neon crossings to Kyoto's bamboo groves, then wind up quiet in the onsen towns near Mount Fuji. Book the Shinkansen early, pack for four seasons, and leave room in the itinerary for whichever side street smells the best.
